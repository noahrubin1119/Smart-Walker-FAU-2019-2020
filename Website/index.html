Introducing the Smart Walker

A walker with integrated sensors and feedback for obstacle avoidance.

  • Learn More

The Problem

Jean Depriso, a resident at Sonata, and other residents sometimes walk into walls or obstacles while using their walkers. We wanted to create a walker with the ability to scan for objects in its path and alert the user.

The walker we used for our base of the design is the same model that Jean Deprisco uses, and Jean was chosen because she is a good communicator and would be able to give feedback.

The Smart Walker

This year, the goal was to complete a project that has real-world applications. After Noah visited Sonata, a local nursing home, and discovered some of the problems patients were having, he informed our team about how some of the patients are constantly walking into walls or over obstacles with their walkers. As a group, we decided to tackle this problem.

We had the idea of building a 'Smart Walker', or a walker that could potentially stop these patients from running into these obstacles with their walkers.

Limitations due to COVID19 and Version 2

Once COVID-19 hit, it was hard, at first, to physically work on and communicate about the project, but it was an issue that was gotten around quickly.

Due to COVID-19, we were not able to meet with Jean at Sonata like we wanted to, so we weren't able to itterate and impove our design. Our goal is eventually to be able to give her the walker so she can test it out, and then continue to work and improve it once that is done.

We would also like to add vibrating motors, add 6-8 sensors and sensors on the sides of the walker, and make the PVC Pipe on the front more subtle.
