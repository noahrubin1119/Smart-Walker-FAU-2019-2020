Smart Walker

Background

This year, the goal was to complete a project that could have more real-world applications. Noah went to Sonata, a local nursing home, and he told us of the main problems that some of the patients were having. He informed us about how some of the patients are constantly walking into walls or over obstacles with their walkers, so as a group, we decided to tackle this problem.

We had the idea of building a 'Smart Walker', or a walker that could potentially stop these patients from running into these obstacles with their walkers.

The approach we took to building this project was based on 3 steps of success.

  1. Make the walker beep when the patient got too close to an obstacle.
  2. Get the walker to break when the patient got too close to an obstacle
  3. Get the walker to beep/vibrate more frequently as the patient got closer and closer to the obstacle.

We collaberated and managed our tasks using a Trello Board - a virtual bulletin board. Everyone on the team created a mini-project with an HC-SR04 Ultrasonic Sensor to learn basic Arduino skills, and after COVID-19 stopped development of the project, everyone completed an Onshape course to better prepare us for next year.

Because we took this multi-step approach, we were able to finish the project's MVP (Minimum Viable Product) so that the first level of success works, but we were unable to complete the second and third levels of success. This means that our project still accomplished our goal of helping the patient with their problem of running into obstacles, it just wasn't as sophisticated as we originally wanted it to be due to our shortened time table.

3D Design

We used 3D printing and itterative design to design the walker's front bar. The bar is a PVC pipe cut according to our CAD Drawing and we attached mounts for Ultrasonic sensors at 4 locations on the bar. We also designed a piece to mount this bar to the walker. We later used Zip Ties to help hold the bar upright on each end of the bar.

Our initial sensor mount design

Final design: squeezing sensor between pipe and 3D printed part, and added an extra bolt hole.

Front bar to walker connection, we used 2 of these - mounting them with screws into the PVC at a fixed angle and with bolts to clamp around walker.
